methodology & limits

Benchmarks measure ceiling under controlled conditions. This measures what users post when they're upset in public forums. They are not the same thing.

1. Selection-biased by construction

The dataset is mentions on Hacker News, r/ClaudeAI, r/OpenAI, r/ChatGPT, r/LocalLLaMA, and r/singularity. People who are angry post; people who are satisfied don't. The chart is a complaint distribution, not a satisfaction metric. The headline cannot be "users hate model X" — it can only be "this is what the loudest critics report this week."

2. Defection language is rhetoric, not behavior

"Cancelled," "switching to," "done with" are tracked because they're high-signal sentiment. They do not measure who actually churned. People announce departures and stay. We have no usage-data ground truth. The chart label says "% of mentions used defection language," not "% of users defected."

3. Two-horse race tracks release calendars

Most short-term variation in this chart is "days since the last contentious release." When OpenAI ships GPT-N, r/OpenAI explodes. Same for Claude. To compare across models without launch confounding, toggle the x-axis to weeks since release.

4. Same regex, both models

The classifier applies identical patterns to claude and openai content. No per-model tuning. The taxonomy was clustered by Claude and Codex independently and reconciled, with a third-vendor model checking for blind spots.

5. Twitter/X is missing

Free-tier API access closed in 2023 and the paid tier doesn't fit the project's zero-recurring-cost rule. Bluesky covers a sliver of similar discourse but is journalist-skewed. This is the loudest gap in the dataset.

6. The corpus volume is asymmetric

ChatGPT/Codex are mentioned far more than Claude. All numbers on this chart are per 1k mentions — never raw counts. Compare the rates, not the bars' absolute heights.
